# Code of Conduct

System Trace is a small open-source project, and we want it to be a friendly
place to contribute - whether you are filing your first issue, sending a typo
fix, or porting a watcher to a new OS.

## The short version

- **Be kind and respectful.** Assume good intent. Disagree about ideas, not
  about people.
- **Be patient.** Maintainers and contributors are volunteers working in their
  own time.
- **Be clear.** When reporting bugs or proposing changes, share enough context
  (OS, version, steps, logs) that someone else can follow along.
- **Stay on topic.** Issues and pull requests are for System Trace - the
  desktop app, its docs, and its website.

## What is not okay

- Personal attacks, harassment, insults, or demeaning remarks.
- Sexual or violent language and imagery.
- Discrimination or targeting someone based on personal characteristics.
- Sharing someone's private information without their permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, issues, or pull requests.

This applies in every project space: the GitHub repositories, issues, pull
requests, discussions, the website, and any direct communication tied to the
project.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that breaks these rules, please email the maintainer at
**sanand03072005@gmail.com**. Reports stay private. We will read every report,
take it seriously, and respond.

Possible responses include a private conversation, a request to edit or delete
content, a temporary cool-off period, or - in serious or repeated cases -
removal from the project's spaces.

## Scope and credit

This document is intentionally short and project-specific. It is inspired by
the spirit of the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/),
without copying its full text. If you want a more detailed reference for what
healthy open-source collaboration looks like, the Covenant is a good read.

By participating in System Trace, you agree to follow this code of conduct.
Thanks for helping keep the project welcoming.
